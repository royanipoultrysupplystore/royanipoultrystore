## 16. Things I'd specifically like your judgment on

**Dari word choices that vary by region** (Afghan Dari vs Iranian Farsi):
- "Phone" → I used `تلفن` (Iranian-style). Afghan Dari sometimes uses `تیلیفون`. Which is more natural for the shop's daily use?
- "Notes" → I used `یادداشت‌ها`. Some prefer `ملاحظات`. Your call.
- "Loading..." → I used `در حال بارگیری…`. Common Afghan UI also uses `لطفاً صبر کنید…` (please wait). Preference?
- "Skip" → I used `رد کردن`. Could also be `گذشتن` or just keep "Skip" in English. What feels right?
- "Driver" → I used `راننده`. Afghan Dari also uses `ډریور` (loanword from English/Pashto). Pick one.
- "Choza" → I used `چوچه` for Dari (chick). Pashto uses `چوزه`. Confirm `چوچه` is right for Dari speakers?

**Phrasing style:**
- For action buttons, Dari can be either imperative (`اضافه کنید` = "add it") or noun (`افزودن` = "adding"). I mixed them based on context — let me know if you'd prefer all-imperative or all-noun for consistency.
